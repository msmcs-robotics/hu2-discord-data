[Beginner Investing Principles] 1. Price is the most important factor to use in relation to value. 2. Try to establish the value of the company. Remember that a share of stock represents a part of a business and is not just a digital receipt. 3. Have patience. Nothing moves immediately. 4. Find the time horizon that suits your personality. This will make adherence to your strategy easier. 5. Don't buy on tips or for a quick move, by the time you’ve heard the rumor its likely already over. 6. Don't be afraid to be a loner but be sure that you are correct in your judgment. You can't be 100% certain but try to look for weaknesses in your thinking. 7. Sell into strength. 8. Have the courage of your convictions once you have made a decision. 9. Have a philosophy of investment and try to follow it. 10. Try to buy assets at a discount than to buy earnings. Earnings can change dramatically in a short time. Usually, assets change slowly. 11. One has to know much more about a company if one buys earnings. 12. Listen to suggestions from people you respect. This doesn't mean you have to accept them. Remember, it's your money and generally, it is harder to keep money than to make it. Once you lose a [lot] of money it is hard to make it back. 13. Try not to let your emotions affect your judgment. Fear and greed are probably the worst emotions to have in connection with the purchase and sale of stocks. 14. Remember the word compounding. For example, if you can make 12% a year and reinvest the money back, you will double your money in six years; taxes excluded. Remember the rule of 72. Your rate of return into 72 will tell you the number of years to double your money. 15. Be careful of leverage. It can go against you.

Got a DM from one of the students on how to go about swing trading and long term portfolio management where one doesn't have to be too active. This is the advice I gave him which I believe others can benefit from as well: - Look at only growth stocks universe. Those are the stocks you want to be in. Those are the ones that will be the Amazon and FB of tomorrow. They will provide you with the mind blowing compounding of your capital. - Be on the lookout for consolidation on a larger time frame. Think of box strategy on weekly/monthly timeframe. If you see a multiweek/multimonth consolidation on a growth stock like Roku, snap, and such, get in on the box break and hold it with conviction. - Have a moving average filter for the indices. For example, if spy breaks below 200dma, go to all cash. The 200dma break suggests that we might be heading for a bear market and you don't want to be invested in such cases. Think of amzn in 2001 or 2008 crash These three things will take you a long way in terms of investing for maximal growth with minimal effort. (edited)
