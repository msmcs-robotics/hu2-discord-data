---------------- 3. Why should I only stick with boxes? I will explain this with a baseball analogy. In baseball, you only swing when the ball is in your strike range. You don't care about the rest. For the most part, you're happy with singles. If you get a home run, that's even better. Now, you can't control when you will get a home run. But markets provide you with unlimited pitches. So, there is no rush. Boxes are your strike zone. You take those trades to keep hitting singles. Once in a while you will get a home run and that's great. However, you shouldn't care about anything that's not in the stike zone. Don't waste your energy. Be focused. ---------------- 4. I struggle with stop losses. What should I do? If you are trading stocks, put an order where the initial stop should be and forget about it. Have a strategy for a trailing stop once your position is profitable. This is covered in the swing trading zoom call. ---------------- 5. How do I deal with greed? When markets are good, I keep increasing my risk and suffer huge drawdowns. Decide on a specific risk per trade. Ideally it should not be more than 1% of your portfolio when starting out. Additionally use 9ma on indexes as a filter If you're a short term trader, stop taking new trades once $spy price falls below 9ma on the daily timeframe. If you're a swing trader with time horizon of a few weeks/months, stop taking any new trades once $spy price falls below 9ma on weekly timeframe. Once price falls below 9ma, tighten your stops and cut most unprofitabe trades. If you trade long options, expect to lose a big chunk of option value by the time the stop hits. This is why you should only buy options with the total amount you're willing to risk. If $spy is way above 9ma, reduce your option buying. (edited)

---------------- 6. I struggle with indecisiveness and fear. What can I do? This usually happens if you're risking too much trying to get rich quick. If you risk small amounts, this won't be an issue. Stop trying to go for home runs. They're a bonus not the standard. Take all box breakouts with a small amount of risk and over time you will be profitable. Poker players don't aim for a full house every time. Most hands are won with a single pair. Small profits add up faster than you realize. ---------------- 7. I don't have enough money to make a living with stocks. What can I do? If you have a small account, a 200% return on a year is stellar. Going for more than that you will be taking excessive risk. If you're trading with anything less than $20k, don't have stocks as your only source of income. Work on building other avenues of cashflow while you learn to trade stocks on the side. Learn sales, freelancing, whatever to have cash consistently coming in. ---------------- 8. When should I take profits? This depends heavily on your objectives and is discused in the swing trading zoom call.

---------------- 12. What should be my trading actions based on the price-alert? I will answer with the help of an example from the price-alert channel. "RTX Crossing 91.51 Break and hold above 91.8 can take RTX to 99.5, then 102. Be mindful of resistances at 93.5, 95.5, and 98" Let's break down this alert - Break and hold above 91.8 => That's your entry to go long. Let the price go above 91.8 and wait for a 5/15 min candle close above that price. If the price doesn't fall below 91.8 immediately, the trade is on. For short trades, it will say something like break and hold below xyz - can take RTX to 99.5 => That's your final target - resistances at 93.5, 95.5, and 98 => These are the important price levels during the trade where you should pay attention. At these levels, I suggest taking partial profits and moving the stop higher to the previous important level. For example, if price reaches 93.5, I will move the stop to right below entry. If price reaches 95.5, I will move the stop to below 93.5. This will help you lock in profits in case of price reversals. The aforementioned example did not have an initial stop but I will add that for future alerts. I have also discussed this structure during the Jan 15 QnA. Go check out that video if you haven't already. (edited)

- Options flow commands What are option flows? Option flows simply tell you the time and sales of the option trades happening. If options flows are heavy to the call side, it means that most market participants are expecting the stock to rise and vice versa. Extremes in options flows usually lead to significant tops and bottoms. 1) tr-bigflow SYMBOL It shows you the largest 10 option flow orders for the day. Usually shows the bias of the instiutional traders. 2) tr-flowsum SYMBOL Shows you the summary statistics of option flow for the given ticker. 3) tr-unusualflow SYMBOL Lists top 10 orders where volume is unusually higher than open interest. If you see a lot of unusual flow from one institution, beware of getting into a position for that stock. That institution can singlehandedly move the market when it unloads its position. 4) tr-topflow Shows the top 10 most bullish and bearish stocks of the day based on options flow. 5) tr-flowheatmap SYMBOL d/w(Optional) Creates a heatmap for top strikes and expirations with premium statistics. Second parameter is the whether you want to create the heatmap for today's data only (d), or for the last 5 days (w). Default is d. 6) tr-whalewatch SYMBOL Shows a visual summary of how & where whales have been building options positions over the last 2-3 weeks. 7) tr-oi SYMBOL EXPIRATION/ALL Shows you open interest by strike and expiration. 8) tr-iv SYMBOL Draws the implied volatility and IV rank of the last 90 days.

- Darkpool Commands What are Darkpools? Darkpools are private exchanges for trading securities that are not accessible to the public. You should care about them since institutions like to accumulate positions through darkpools in order to keep their exposure hidden from public. 1) tr-dplevels SYMBOL Shows you the darkpool levels from recent DP data. These levels should usually correspond with important price zones (support/resistances) on the chart. 2) tr-darkpool SYMBOL Lists out the last several big darkpool trades. 3) tr-largestdp Shows you the largest darkpool orders across all stocks. 4) tr-largestbt Shows you the largest block trade orders across all stocks. - Stock Search Commands 1) tr-srlevels SYMBOL PERIOD(d/m/h/w) Draws out support and resistance levels on the price chart. 2) tr-insider SYMBOL : Lists out the latest insider trades for the stock. Insider selling doesn't mean much. Insiders sell stocks to pay for all kinds of stuff. Significant insider buying, however, deserves your interest. 3) tr-news SYMBOL Shows you the latest news for the ticker. 4) tr-analysts SYMBOL Lists out the latest analyst upgrades and downgrades for the company. If a company is strong 5) tr-chatter SUBREDDIT/TWITTER Shows you the most discussed stocks across various subreddits and twitter. Those should be the commands you stick to. If you have questions about how to use any of them, feel free to ask in chat. To view all the other commands, use tr-help.
