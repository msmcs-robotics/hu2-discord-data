Welcome to technical-analysis room. Technical analysis is a way of understanding the markets that relies on one simple principle Mantra 1: The price of a product will tell you everything you need to know how to bet profitably on it. No need for company data, news, CEO's marriage, whatever. The price will tell us everything we need to know and our decisions will be solely based on it. How does this help? We remove the need for guesswork and trade a simple plan. We have an entry and an exit. Based on the exit, we decide the maximum amount we are willing to lose on the trade and size accordingly. Mantra 2: The market has two phases aka trending and consolidating (non-trending) Our goal is to identify when the market is consolidating so we can enter with a defined risk. These are the times when the average gunslinger investor is bored by this stock and is looking elsewhere. We get in and once the market starts moving and the other investors enter, we sell the stock to others at a higher price and wait again. Our aim for this room will be to identify these consolidation patterns, study them and understand them well to extract the best reward to risk for our trades.

Price Swing Market Structure 101 Bullish Market Structure – Series of higher highs (HH) and higher lows (HL). Can be seen in the price action as resistance breaking and turning into support. A higher low is where people would “buy the dip” in hopes to hit or exceed the previous price swing for a profit. Bearish Market Structure – Series of lower highs (LH) and lower lows (LL). Can be seen in the price action as support breaking and turning into resistance. Rangebound Market Structure – Can also be known as consolidation or sideways market. This is a series of equal highs (EH) and equal lows (EL). Price is bouncing between a support and resistance. One way to play rangebound markets is to long the bottom of the range or short the top of the range, take profit when price hits the opposite side of the range. If you want to trade with a high probability of a successful outcome, trade in the direction of the trend, hence the saying “the trend is your friend.” It is not recommended for beginners to make counter trend trades. Relative Strength among Securities The idea for this topic came from a discussion in the chat this week where QQQ was suposedly leading the move down against SPY on one of the trading days. A student expressed interest on trying to use that relation for some trading decisions. My rebuttal for the idea was that it's hard to detemine when such a relation stops working. There is a nuance to my rebuttal though. It's only hard to detemine when that relation stops working if we have no context for it. Hence, today I will show you how to assess the relative strength between any two securities. It can be - index vs index (QQQ vs SPY) - stock vs index (AAPL vs SPY) - commodity vs index (GOLD vs SPY) - commodity vs commodity (GOLD vs SILVER) or any other combination you can think of. (edited)

I have taken an example on the weekly timeframe to show you the use of boxes in determining relative strength between two securities. However, such relations also work on other timeframes and you're more than welcome to explore them. In the example above, you can see that QQQ/SPY created a massive 1.5 year box between May 2018 and Oct 2019. During this 1.5 year period, you can't designate either QQQ or SPY as the leader. You can say that they were evenly matched. Once the relative price broke out of the box, a trend began in the relative price such that QQQ was the leader going forward. The trend continued from Oct 2019 to Feb 2021 where the trend structure finally broke. QQQ/SPY is back in a box now. If you look at the daily chart during the trend period, you will see plenty more boxes that developed. They would appear as sideways consolidation on the weekly timeframe as 9ma caught up with the relative price. I would urge you to draw some boxes on relative charts and see the trending and non-trending periods for yourself. Once you know how to check the relative strength between securities, you need to think of how to use it best.

It is used to determine the best security universe for your capital. For example, let's say QQQ/SPY is trending higher. That means that QQQ is currently stronger of the two. Once you have detemined that, there is no reason to be putting your hard earned cash in SPY for the time being. Now, lets say SOCL (social media ETF)/QQQ just broke out of a box. Hence, the best place for your cash out of all tech would be social media stocks. This is how you use relative strength between securities to narrow down the entire security universe to a few securities which would be the best bang for your buck.
